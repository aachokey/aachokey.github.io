About

I'm Aric, a digital storyteller, data journalist and inherent creator of copy and code.


My most recent gig was with the Associated Press in Indiana as a legislative relief reporter writing stories with both state and national scope. For nearly 5 months, I skulked around the Statehouse cornering politicians and tracking daily legislative actions for the wire. I also got a chance to cover the state primaries and presidential elections and investigated shady campaign contributions to the state's governor's race.


In my experience so far, I've had a chance to wear a few hats in digital communication building web interfaces, copy editing, news editing, beat and graphic reporting and social media strategy.


I've spent the previous few years melding reporting with web development to help my storytelling process through writing web scrapers in Python, manipulating data for analysis on a command line and visualizing intangible datasets.


I spend a lot of time reading about investigative journalism, UX design and front-end methodologies. I usually tweet a lot of the ideas and stories I come across, so connect with me on Twitter and see which ideas I'm tossing around.
