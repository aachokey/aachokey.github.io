Aric A. Chokey

Hi, I'm Aric.

I am currently the news editor at the Ball State Daily News. The majority of my job is helping reporters reach their potential by coaching them with story angles, structuring writing, cultivating sources and digesting complicated topics. I've also helped carry the section through our newspaper's online-first transition.

Aside from editing work, I keep my reporting and writing skills sharp as a politics reporter. I wrangle political events and moves and repackage them to relate to Ball State.

My interests are mainly in data journalism, coding and creating stories that are useful to the public. Outside of class and the Daily News, I'm always working on learning a new computer language and methods to web scraping and data manipulation.

I'm in my last semester as a journalism and telecommunications major at Ball State University.

I've had the opportunity to get some writing experience from a few different professional gigs.


Ball State Daily News

Chief reporter, copy editor

I've covered diverse topics as a writer for the Daily News including community development, depression on campus, diversity and racism, university administration, fraud and even President Obama. As a beat reporter, I learned to cultivate sources, dissect documents and produce photo, video and interactive maps to showcase different facets of stories.

The South Bend Tribune

Reporting intern

I spent 10 weeks as an intern writing stories about community initiatives and business at the publication with a 65,000 daily circulation. To go along with my stories, I also got to create interactive maps and hung out with beat reporters as they chased stories and mentored me on beat reporting.

Indiana State Senate

Communications intern

With a caseload of five senators, I spent the 2014 legislative session as a communications intern turning public data into digestible information for legislators, researching talking points for senators, tailoring press releases, managing media interviews and producing photo, video, graphic and podcasts for legislative marketing material.
